PRo3D Getting Started Technology



PRo3D, short for Planetary Robotics 3D Viewer, is an interactive 3D visualization tool to allow planetary scientists to work with high-resolution 3D reconstructions of the Martian surface.

Who uses PRo3D?

PRo3D aims to support planetary scientists in the course of NASA's and ESA's missions to find signs of life on the red planet by exploring high resolution digital surface reconstructions from obiter and rover cameras.

For the past 5 years development of PRo3D has been geared towards providing planetary geologists with interactive tools to digitize geological features on digital outcrop models (DOMs) on the Martian surface. During our fruitful cooporation with geologists from the Imperial College of London PRo3D has emerged as their main tool to conduct remote geological which lead to many publications and talks at various geological science venues.

Planetary geology is the most elaborately supported use-case of PRo3D, however we strive to expand our user groups to other use-cases, so we have also developed features geared towards supporting science goals in landing site selection and mission planning.
